1. Recognize Digital Advertising Is Fast Changing

Between 2010 and 2022, the percentage of the world population using the Internet more than doubled from 28.9% to 63.1%. Advertising agencies ported advertising pipelines from traditional advertising channels, like newspapers, magazines, bill-boards, and direct mail, to digital advertising, or advertising made for the electronic devices global consumers now use 7 hours on average everyday.



Unlike traditional advertising, digital advertising is made for technologies that evolve incredibly fast. Newly released devices give consumers opportunities to explore new media channels and software. And they do. Instantaneous global communication comes with fast moving trends. From 2017 to 2022, Tik Tok’s revenue grew more than 74.5 times. In the same time period, the global industry for location data grew to 14 billion USD. It’s expected to double by 2028.

Digital advertising firms evolved rapidly to keep pace. 86% of firms added a new service offering in 2021.

While social media advertising revenues have soared relative other agency revenue streams, today’s digital advertising agencies perform many valuable services.

3. Implement a Digital Marketing Strategy

According to Media Valet, 45% of companies don’t have a digital marketing strategy. Companies that have a strategy and document it are 313% more likely to show success.

Strategizing prompts organizations to assess what’s working and what isn’t. They can then make changes and align teams to intelligently focus forward before iterating back through marketing cycles, re-strategizing, re-planning, and re-implementing.

During the pandemic, companies like Walmart, Amazon, Ebay, and Alibaba grew their online sales. Others, like, Pinduoduo didn’t.

While many business factors impact profitability, especially in recession, successful firm operations begin and end with strategy.

Chapters, Canada’s behemoth book retailer, is a great example of a firm that introduced and implemented a successful digital marketing strategy, through the pandemic, to grow their online sales ~180%, 5 times more than the companies mentioned above during the same time period.

The benefits gained from optimally strategizing are just too large to overlook, which is why it’s surprising so many companies don’t have a digital marketing strategy.

4. Design a Digital Marketing Plan Like the Pros

Digital marketing strategy informs digital marketing plans.

Plans leverage changing information, typically extracted from marketing data collected across sectors and analyzed by specialty firms, like CT21 Analytics.

Over the short-run, with an intelligent plan in place, firms can re-target marketing goals and guide business units to better navigate business environments.

A digital marketing plan might touch on everything from the brand itself, as represented in logos, colour palettes, slogans, and images, to the website design, website performance, and even specialized implementations of the website that evolve from testing to improve search engine optimization and user experience.

Plans should also be informed with case study research, to better understand how successful companies shape sales in the era of new digital technologies. Fortunately, public companies publish plans annually.

5. Keep Your Marketing Plan Agile. Re-strategize, Re-plan, And Re-Implement Frequently

In software, short-cycle development and execution ensure timely stable product, version, and update releases. The same project management principles can be applied to marketing cycles.

Changing advertising trends, economic cycles, demographic preferences, and consumer spending habits demand agile marketing strategy, plan, and implementation.

Again, according to Media Valet, more than 45% of companies don’t have a long-term and short-term digital marketing strategy.

Maintaining a long-term vision, based on organizational principles and higher-level goals, keeps short-term planning on point. While consistent iteration across short-term marketing cycles ensures new information derived from analytics and research informs operational direction.

To adequately leverage digital marketing services, ensure planning incorporates both short-term and long-term principles, information, and vision.

6. Know the Difference Between Brand Marketing And Brand Power

Elon Musk’s famous 2014 statement, ’All Our Patent Are Belong To You,’ was an endorsement of open source technology and a public declaration that Tesla wasn’t afraid of the competition.

But the brazen move to ’not initiate patent lawsuits’ against ’anyone who, in good faith’ wanted to use their technology, was also a confident endorsement of the Tesla brand.

Today Tesla’s solid hold on the economy luxury car market niche is as good an example of brand power as any. It’s as if they knew something the rest of the market didn’t.

Brand power is a measure of brand recognition and reputation that generates economic monopoly power. It’s important for firms to recognize the difference between the power in a brand and brand marketing, which uses that power to build relationships with customers and improve customer retention and satisfaction.

Powerful brands can set higher pricing points, they’re more resilient to market dynamics, and they grow faster.
